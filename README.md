# 100 Days of Cloud DevOps Challenge

Welcome to my **100 Days of Cloud DevOps Challenge**!

For the next **100 days**, I’m committing **at least one hour every day** to building, learning, and practicing Cloud and DevOps skills.

This journey will be **intensely hands-on and thoroughly documented**. My goal is to make everything reproducible—anyone following along should be able to recreate the projects and environments from their own home lab.

My setup will combine **Raspberry Pi devices for local infrastructure** and **AWS for cloud services**, creating a practical hybrid environment for experimentation, automation, deployment, and operations.

Why am I doing this?

Because one of the best ways to truly learn is to **build, share, and teach others**. By documenting the process publicly, I hope to strengthen my own understanding while helping others who are on a similar path.

This challenge may be called **100 Days**, but the goal is much bigger: to build habits, deepen expertise, and continue long after day 100.

## Rules

1. I will push my work to GitHub daily so others can follow my progress and see what I’m building.

2. I will maintain and update my **Activity Log** (`log.md`) each day with progress notes and links to completed work.

3. I will stay consistent, focus on continuous improvement, and document lessons learned throughout the challenge.

4. I will prioritize hands-on practice and real-world Cloud DevOps projects whenever possible.

---

## Goal

Build discipline, strengthen Cloud and DevOps skills, create a public portfolio of consistent progress, and develop projects that others can learn from and reproduce.
